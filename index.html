Here's a breakdown of what influences the production rate:

Complexity of the Doll:

  • Simple Rag Doll: A very basic, unjointed rag doll with simple embroidered features and felt hair will be much faster to construct.
  • Detailed Doll: A doll with more complex body parts, articulated limbs, rooted yarn hair, or intricate facial embroidery will take considerably longer.

Complexity of the Outfit:

  • Simple Dress/Thobe: A one-piece simple dress or a basic doll thobe with minimal embellishment is quick.
  • Multi-Piece Ensemble: A traditional outfit with multiple layers (e.g., underdress, overdress, headscarf, accessories), intricate embroidery (like Thobe al-Nashal), tiny buttons, or delicate fabrics will slow down production immensely.
  • Embellishments: Hand-sewn beads, intricate machine embroidery programs, lace application, or unique trim require extra time.

Tailor's Skill and Experience:

  • An experienced tailor specializing in doll making will be much faster and more efficient than someone new to it.
  • Speed and precision vary from person to person.

Tools and Equipment:

  • Machine vs. Hand Sewing: Most doll parts and outfits are machine-sewn for efficiency, but some details (like attaching hair, facial features, or intricate embellishments) might be hand-sewn.
  • Specialized Equipment: Access to industrial sewing machines, good cutting tools, and efficient workstations can boost productivity.
  • Pre-cut Pieces: If fabric pieces are already precisely cut using a die-cutter or a quick method, it saves significant time compared to the tailor cutting each piece manually.

Production Method:

  • Batch Production: A tailor might work more efficiently by doing all the "heads" first, then all the "bodies," then all the "outfits," rather than completing one doll at a time from start to finish.
  • Assembly Line: If you scale up and have different people doing different steps (e.g., one person cuts, another sews bodies, another sews outfits, another does finishing), the overall output per 8 hours will drastically increase, but that's a team effort, not a single tailor.

Quality Standards:

  • Higher quality demands more meticulous work, which naturally takes more time.

Rough Estimates (Per 8-hour workday, for a single tailor):

  • Very Simple Doll + Simple Outfit: A highly efficient tailor might produce 5-10 dolls (e.g., basic rag doll with a simple sewn dress/pajama).
  • Medium Complexity Doll + Medium Outfit: (e.g., your standard Bahraini/Modern play doll with one outfit) A more realistic estimate for a skilled tailor might be 2-4 dolls.
  • Highly Complex Doll + Intricate Outfit: (e.g., a "Thobe al-Nashal" doll or a wedding doll with many details) This could be 1 doll, or even less than 1 doll (e.g., 0.5-1 doll per day, meaning 1 doll takes 8-16 hours).

To get an accurate number for your specific production:

The best way is to do a time trial yourself or with your tailor. Make 2-3 dolls of each complexity level (simple, medium, complex) from start to finish, meticulously timing each step. This will give you a much more realistic production rate for your unique products and processes.
